1. Summary

1.1 What is Virtual Survivor?

Welcome to Virtual Survivor, a social game for terrible people.

Based off of the popular reality TV show by CBS, Survivor, this is a social game involving various challenges and games, relationship building, alliance forming, and backstabbing. Specifically, this is a virtual adaptation of Survivor, aiming to recreate all the main aspects of the show through your browser so that anyone can play from anywhere!

2. Game Setup Number of Players, Prizes, Discord

2.1 Number of Players

Games have a minimum of 12 players and maximum of 20 players.
Number of players will determine lengths of the various Game Stages, defined later in section 3.1. The creator of the Game will be known as the Game Admin.

2.2 Prizes

Up to the Admin and agreed upon by all participants.
Typically, we suggest having a monetary buy-in amount and dividing the total prize pool as follows:

  • 3rd Place: Buy-in amount
  • 2nd Place: 2x buy-in amount
  • 1st Place: Remainder of prize pool

2.3 Discord

Prior to creating the game, the Admin should create a Discord Server with the following channels:

  • [Text Channel] Information
  • [Text Channel] Tribe 1
  • [Text Channel] Tribe 2
  • [Voice Channel] General
  • [Voice Channel] Tribe 1
  • [Voice Channel] Tribe 2

Note that the Tribe channels should only be accessible to players of that tribe.
The Admin will be required to input the link to the created Discord Server when creating the Game, as to allow players to join the server.

3. Game Format Game Stages

3.1 Game Stages

Games consist of the following Stages, each comprised of Rounds:

  • Tribes (including Tribe Swaps)
  • Merge
  • Final 4 Round
  • Final 3 Round

The length of each game stage is as follows:

  • If 18-20 players:
    • Games start with players divided into 2 Tribes
    • Tribe Swap #1 when there are 15 players remaining
    • Tribe Swap #2 when there are 12 players remaining
    • Merge into one joint tribe when one tribe drops to 4 players
    • Final 4 Round will begin once there are 4 players remaining
    • Final 3 Finale Round will begin once the 4th place person is eliminated
  • If 14-17 players:
    • Games start with players divided into 2 Tribes
    • Tribe Swap when there are 12 players remaining
    • Merge into one joint tribe when one tribe drops to 4 players
    • Final 4 Round will begin once there are 4 players remaining
    • Final 3 Finale Round will begin once the 4th place person is eliminated
  • If 12-13 players:
    • Games start with players divided into 2 Tribes
    • Merge into one joint tribe when one tribe drops to 4 players
    • Final 4 Round will begin once there are 4 players remaining
    • Final 3 Finale Round will begin once the 4th place person is eliminated

3.2 Tribes

The initial player pool will be randomly divided into two Tribes.
In the case of an odd number of players, one Tribe will have an additional player.
Tribes will compete against each other in Rounds, as described in section 3.7. The losing Tribe will then go to Tribal Council (4.2) to vote on one player to eliminate from the Game.

3.3 Tribe Swaps

When a Tribe Swap occurs, Tribes will be randomly shuffled to produce two new Tribes.

3.4 Merge

When the Merge occurs, all players will be combined into one single Tribe.

3.5 Final 4 Round

When there are 4 players remaining, there will be a special Final 4 Challenge between the remaining players.
The Winner of the Final 4 Challenge will win Immunity and a guaranteed spot in the Final 3 Finale Round.
The Winner will select one other person among the Final 4 to join them in the Final 3 Finale Round. The remaining two players will compete in the Tower Challenge (best of 3) to determine who will join the previous 2 players in the Final 3 Finale Round.

NOTE: It is possible for the Winner to choose 2 players among the Final 4 to be in the Final 3 Finale Round and participate in the Tower Challenge themselves.

3.6 Final 3 Round

To determine the winner, in the Final 3 Finale Round, there will be no Challenge. Instead, eliminated players will form the Jury and vote on who they think the Game winner is.
The Final 3 players will have an equal amount of time (i.e 2 minutes) to plead their case to the Jury, followed by a Jury Questioning Period where anyone in the Jury can ask any or all of the Final 3 any question they desire. Once there are no further questions, the Jury will vote for a 1st place winner and 2nd place winner. The player with the highest number of 1st place votes is the Game Winner, and in the case of a tie, the number of 2nd place votes will be taken into account.

4. Rounds Tribal Council, Challenges, Voting Rules, Scheduling

4.1 What is a Round?

The Game is played thorugh numbered Rounds, which consist of:

  • Tribal Council
  • Challenge
  • Idol Drop

Between Rounds, players are encouraged to communicate between other players to strategize.

4.3 Tribal Council

During Tribal Council, the associated Players will each cast 1 vote on which player they wish to eliminate from the Game.
Prior to voting, Players will all discuss and reflect upon the Game thus far, with topics including but not limited to:

  • Past Challenges
  • Last Round's Challenge
  • Relationships and Alliances
  • Idols in the Game
  • The Other Tribe
  • How this Tribal Council Impacts the Game
  • Your Game Thus Far

4.4 Voting Rules

  • Players may only vote for 1 player to eliminate
  • Players with Immunity Idols may announce if they wish to use one AFTER all votes are cast, but before voting results are revealed
  • If a player is voted out, they will join the Jury and will vote for the final winner among the final 3 during the Final 3 Round
  • In the case of a Tie, another vote will be conducted where players may only vote between those involved in the Tie
  • If there is still a Tie, those not among the players NOT being voted upon in the Tie must discuss and UNANIMOUSLY decide on who to eliminate among those Tied Players
  • If no unanimous conclusion is met, the Tied Players are granted Immunity, players during the vote who had Immunity still retain that status, and all other players will be placed in a pool where one random player will be selected to be eliminated

4.5 Challenges

Challenges will be randomly drawn from a pool and can be played virtually or in real-life. The winning Tribe or Player will be granted Immunity and will not be able to be voted out during the following Tribal Council.

4.6 Idol Drop

To conclude Rounds, each player will be given an opportunity to select a random number between 1-12 (inclusive). A number in that range will be randomly chosen and all players who predicted correctly will be granted 1 Imminity Idol.
Imminity Idols may be played during a Tribal Council (see section 4.4) or given to another player between Rounds.

4.7 Round #1

As this will initiate the Game, there will be no Tribal Council during Round #1.
During this Round, players will be split into two Tribes. Therefore, instead of playing Challenges for Immunity, the Tribes will be playing for one Immunity Idol.
The Immunity Idol will be given to any 1 player that the winning Tribe decides.
The first Challenge will then be played, with the winning Tribe winning Immunity and the losing Tribe attending Tribal Council to begin Round #2.

4.8 Round Scheduling

The Game Admin is in charge of scheduling Rounds. If there is no date that fits everyone's schedules, the Admin will have to make a decision on how to proceed with the next Round.

5. Suggestions and FAQs Final notes

5.1 How should I communicate to other players?

For communication between Tribes, we recommend using the corresponding channels located in your Game's official Discord Server. For individual or alliance communications, the Game page on the web application provides each player's Discord username.

5.2 What do I do after I've been eliminated?

You can still join Rounds, though you cannot participate in Challenges. The next time you will have an impact on the Game will be during the Final 3 Finale Round in which you will be a part of the Jury.

5.3 Game Integrity

Although the final decision is made by the Admin, for the sake of game integrity, we strongly encourage the following:

  • NO BRIBING OR AGREEMENTS INVOLVING GAME PRIZE MONEY
  • NO SENDING SCREENSHOTS OF ANY CHATS OR MESSAGES BETWEEN PLAYERS
  • NO FORGING SCREENSHOTS OR MESSAGES BETWEEN PLAYERS

5.4 What do I do if I need help/clarification about the game?

Contact one of the Game Designers: Joseph [Joseph#2844], Thomas [Spyral#9645], or Isaac [IZeke#2190].

5.5 What do I do if I think I ran into a bug on the site?

Contact one of the Game Developers: Thomas [Spyral#9645] or JJ [Sir Cartier#8906].

Remember that this game involves backstabbing and scheming, but at the end of the day, this is all FOR FUN. Back stabbing is a part of the game, do don't get too hurt when it happens.
